A website that actually converts.

Most Bayside tradie websites look fine and do nothing. Here's what actually makes the difference between a website that generates enquiries and one that sits there looking average.

M
Michael Beets
Founder & Director, Beets & Co

Most tradie websites fail not because they look bad, but because they were built to look good rather than to convert. The designer focused on visual appeal. Nobody asked: what does a Bayside homeowner actually need to see in order to pick up the phone?

The answers aren't complicated. But most tradie sites get them wrong. Here are the five things that actually make the difference.

A homeowner in Cheltenham deciding who to call doesn't read your website carefully. They scan it in under 10 seconds to answer one question: "Can I trust this person to turn up and do the job right?"

Five things Bayside tradies actually need

01

Phone number above the fold

On mobile — which is where 70–80% of local trade searches happen — the phone number needs to be immediately visible. Not in the footer. Not two scrolls down. In the header, large, clickable. This single change increases inbound calls more than almost anything else.

02

Suburb-specific pages

A single homepage that says "serving all of Melbourne" ranks for nothing specifically. Dedicated pages for each suburb you work in — Cheltenham, Mentone, Parkdale, Mordialloc — each with unique content about that area, are what rank in local search and signal genuine local presence to Google.

03

Fast load times

Google uses page speed as a ranking factor. More importantly, a page that takes 4 seconds to load on mobile loses a significant percentage of its visitors before they even see your content. Most tradie websites — particularly those built on slow page builders — fail this test badly. Fix it.

04

Real trust signals

Google reviews displayed on the site. Real photos of completed jobs. Your face and name. Licence numbers where relevant. A specific list of suburbs you serve. These aren't nice to have — they're the difference between a visitor who calls and one who goes back to the results and tries someone else.

Stock photography from a Wix template does the opposite of building trust. Bayside homeowners making $15,000–$50,000 decisions are perceptive. They know what genuine looks like.

05

A single, clear call to action

Most tradie sites have four different CTAs on every page: "Get a Quote," "Call Us," "Book Online," "Send a Message." Confusion kills conversion. Pick the primary action — for most trade businesses that's a phone call or a simple contact form — and make everything point toward it.

The Bayside opportunity

The Bayside corridor — Chelsea, Bonbeach, Edithvale, Aspendale, Mordialloc, Mentone, Cheltenham, Sandringham — is dominated by established, high-spending homeowners. They have the budgets for quality work and they have high expectations for the professionalism of who they hire.

The web presence of most trade businesses in these suburbs is genuinely poor. A properly built, fast, mobile-optimised website with real photos, suburb-specific pages, and visible reviews is not a high bar — but it's one that most local competitors haven't cleared. The tradies who build a serious web presence now will own their suburbs for years.

What a properly built site includes

→Mobile-first design — built for phone screens, not desktop
→Phone number in sticky header — clickable on mobile
→Dedicated suburb + service landing pages
→Real photography — job photos, team, vehicles
→Google review widget with live rating display
→LocalBusiness schema markup
→Page speed under 2.5 seconds on mobile
→Single, clear primary CTA per page
