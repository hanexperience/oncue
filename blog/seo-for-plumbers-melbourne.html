Plumbing has among the highest local search intent of any trade. When a pipe bursts in Cheltenham at 7pm, the homeowner isn't thinking about price — they're thinking about who can come now. They open Google, search "plumber Cheltenham," and they call whoever appears first in the local pack.

There are typically three spots in that local pack. The Melbourne plumber who occupies one of them consistently gets a disproportionate share of inbound work — at zero cost per click. The ones who don't show up either pay for ads or rely on referrals from an increasingly thin network.

Here's how to get there.

The Melbourne plumber who ranks #1 in their suburb typically gets 3–5× more calls than the plumber ranked #4 — even if the quality of work is identical.

The four ranking drivers

01

Google Business Profile

Primary category: "Plumber." Add secondary categories like "Emergency Plumbing Service" if relevant. Service area: every Melbourne suburb you genuinely cover — for most plumbers that's 10–20. Write a 200–300 word description that includes your services and the areas you work in. Upload job photos every fortnight. Post updates monthly at minimum.

02

Reviews

Plumbing is a high-urgency, high-trust purchase. Reviews are the primary trust signal. The Melbourne plumbers ranking in the top 3 of any given suburb typically have 30–60+ reviews with consistent recent additions. Build a system to collect them after every job — text message within 2 hours, direct Google review link.

03

Website with suburb-level pages

One homepage that says "plumber serving Melbourne" won't cut it. Build dedicated pages for your key suburbs and services: "Plumber Cheltenham," "Blocked Drains Mentone," "Hot Water Systems Parkdale." Each page needs a title tag, H1, and genuine local content — not template copy with the suburb name swapped in.

04

NAP consistency

Name, address, phone — identical on your website, GBP, Yellow Pages, True Local, Hipages, and everywhere else you're listed. Inconsistencies confuse Google's local signals. Audit your existing listings and fix any variations before you do anything else.

Melbourne suburbs to target first

For Bayside plumbers: Cheltenham, Mentone, Parkdale, Mordialloc, Aspendale, Edithvale, Chelsea, Carrum. For South-East: Clayton, Oakleigh, Bentleigh, Carnegie, Moorabbin, Highett. Prioritise suburbs where you already have customers and can get reviews from — local proof in a suburb is worth more than technical optimisation without it.

7-point SEO checklist

✓GBP category set to "Plumber" — service area lists every suburb you work in
✓GBP has 20+ real job photos, updated within the last 3 months
✓Review collection system in place — minimum 2 new reviews per month
✓Website has a page per key service + suburb combination
✓Plumber schema markup on homepage
✓NAP consistent across website, GBP, Yellow Pages, True Local, Hipages
✓Website loads under 3 seconds on mobile, phone number above the fold

Ranking timeline: what to expect

Local SEO for Melbourne plumbers typically shows meaningful movement within 3 months for lower-competition suburbs, and 4–6 months for higher-competition areas like Cheltenham or Bentleigh. GBP improvements (more reviews, photo activity, regular posts) often show results faster than website changes.

The compounding effect matters here. A plumber who starts now and stays consistent for 12 months won't just rank — they'll be difficult to displace, because their review volume and recency will be hard to catch.
