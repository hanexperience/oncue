Stop losing leads while you work.

Five marketing automations that save Melbourne trade businesses hours each week — and make sure every lead gets a fast, professional response even when you're on the tools.

M
Michael Beets
Founder & Director, Beets & Co

Most Melbourne tradies are running their business from a phone and a spreadsheet. Leads come in — sometimes — and sometimes they don't get followed up fast enough. A quote goes out and disappears. A call is missed on site and never returned. Reviews aren't being asked for.

Marketing automation doesn't solve all of this. But it handles the five most common failure points automatically — so you stop losing business you should be winning.

The trade businesses pulling ahead in Melbourne aren't working harder — they've built systems that work when they can't.

Five automations worth setting up

01

Instant lead response

When someone fills out a contact form on your website, they get an immediate, personalised acknowledgement by SMS and email — within 60 seconds. No manual action needed. The message confirms their enquiry, sets expectations on timing, and positions your business professionally before you've even seen the lead.

Studies consistently show that leads contacted within the first 5 minutes are dramatically more likely to convert than those contacted an hour later. Your Melbourne competitors are rarely doing this.

02

Missed call text-back

If someone calls you while you're on the tools and you can't answer, they get an automatic text within seconds: "Hi, sorry I missed your call — I'm out on a job right now. I'll call you back within [X] hours. Can you let me know what you need?" Simple. Professional. It captures the lead before they dial the next plumber on the list.

03

Quote follow-up sequence

After a quote is sent, a short automated sequence follows up at day 2 and day 5 if there's no response. Not aggressive — just "checking in to see if you have any questions about the quote." Most tradies send a quote and wait indefinitely. This closes jobs that would otherwise fall through the gaps.

04

Review request after job completion

When a job is marked complete in your system, an automated text goes to the customer with your Google review link and a brief, genuine request. The timing — within hours of the job — maximises the chance they act on it. This is the simplest way to build review volume consistently without remembering to ask every time.

05

AI website chat

An AI chatbot on your website that can answer basic service questions, collect enquiry details, and qualify leads — 24/7. For a Cheltenham plumber, this might handle "do you do emergency callouts?", "what areas do you cover?", and "how do I get a quote?" — turning website visitors into actionable leads even at midnight.

These aren't the clunky chatbots of five years ago. Modern AI chat is conversational and handles nuanced questions without frustrating the user.

A practical example

A plumber in Campbellfield sets up all five automations. On a typical Tuesday, they're on a job from 7am to 4pm.

At 9:15am, a homeowner in Fawkner fills out the contact form. An automated text and email acknowledges the enquiry within a minute. At 11:30am, someone calls — goes to voicemail, and immediately receives a text-back. At 3pm, a quote sent 3 days ago gets a follow-up nudge — the customer replies and confirms. At 4:45pm, the plumber marks a morning job complete in their CRM, and the customer gets a review request SMS.

That's four touch points handled automatically. Without the systems, two of those leads would have gone cold.

Choosing an automation agency

Most automation agencies in Melbourne build systems on top of platforms you've never heard of, charge ongoing monthly fees for access, and hand you a login without any training. When something breaks, you're on your own.

What to look for instead: a partner who understands trade businesses specifically, builds on platforms you actually own or can take with you, and stays involved when you need to adjust the system as your business grows.
