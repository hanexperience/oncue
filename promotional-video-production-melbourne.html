Why most promotional videos don't work

Most businesses that have tried promotional video before didn't get results — and they blame the video. Usually the problem was upstream.

  • No script strategy. The business owner was put on camera and asked to "just talk about what you do." The result is a rambling video nobody watches past the 15-second mark. Good video starts with a brief and a written script, not a camera.
  • Wrong length for the platform. A two-minute video on a homepage can work if it's compelling. The same two-minute video repurposed for Instagram Reels will lose 90% of viewers in the first three seconds. Each platform needs its own cut.
  • No call to action. The video looks nice and ends. The viewer thinks "yeah, they seem good" and closes the tab. Every promotional video needs to direct the viewer to a specific next step — call, enquire, visit.
  • Disconnected from the rest of the marketing. A promo video sitting on a slow, low-converting website won't save the page. Video works when it's part of a system — paired with good copy, the right keywords and a clear enquiry path.

We write the brief, the script and the shot list before a camera is ever turned on. Strategy before production. That's what makes the difference between a video that sits unwatched and one that drives enquiries.

How the process works

Promotional video production at Beets & Co follows a tight, four-stage process. No endless briefing cycles. No surprises on the day.

  • Brief & strategy. We start with a 45-minute call to understand your business, your customer and what the video needs to achieve. We agree on the format, length, platform and call to action before anything else moves.
  • Script & shot list. We write the script and planned shots. You review and approve before we book a shoot day. Nothing is improvised on location.
  • Shoot day in Melbourne. A half or full day on location — your premises, a site, or a suitable Melbourne location. We direct the day, manage the talent, and capture everything on the shot list plus additional cutaway footage.
  • Edit, colour & delivery. We edit the primary cut, colour grade, add music and graphics, and deliver all agreed formats — landscape for the web, portrait for social. Two rounds of revisions included.

Common questions about promotional video

How much does promotional video production cost in Melbourne?

At Beets & Co, promotional video projects are fixed-price. A single promo video typically starts from $2,500 AUD depending on length, shoot complexity and edit requirements. Packages that include multiple cuts — social, website, landscape and portrait — are available. You'll know the full cost before anything is booked.

How long does a promotional video take to produce?

Most projects move from briefing to delivery in 2–4 weeks. This includes a pre-production planning session, a half or full day shoot in Melbourne, editing, colour grading and two rounds of revisions. Rush timelines can be accommodated depending on availability.

What's the difference between a promotional video and a commercial?

A promotional video is typically 60–180 seconds and lives on your website, Google Business Profile or social media. It introduces your business, shows your work and gives a potential customer a reason to call. A commercial is usually shorter, made for broadcast or paid media, and built around a single message or offer. Beets & Co produces both — and often both come from a single shoot day.

Do you only make videos for trade businesses?

Beets & Co primarily works with trade businesses, operators, manufacturers and local Melbourne businesses. If you run a real business that does real things and you want a video that shows it clearly and drives enquiries, we can help. We don't work with personal brands, influencers or event videography.
