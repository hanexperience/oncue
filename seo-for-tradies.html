What tradie SEO actually involves

SEO for a trade business isn't about chasing broad keywords. It's a focused stack of things that work together — each one building on the last.

Suburb targeting

Dedicated pages for every suburb you work in — written to rank for "[trade] [suburb]" searches. The fastest way to appear in Local Pack results across multiple areas.

Google Business Profile

A complete, actively managed profile is the single biggest driver of Local Pack rankings. Primary category, service area, photos, posts, Q&A — all built out properly.

Google review strategy

Volume and recency both drive Local Pack rankings. A simple, repeatable system to collect 2–4 new reviews every month — and build an advantage that compounds over time.

On-page SEO

Title tags, meta descriptions, structured data, NAP consistency, page speed. The technical signals that tell Google what you do, where you do it, and that you're a legitimate local business.

Why most tradie SEO doesn't work

The most common failure is targeting keywords that are too broad. "Electrician Melbourne" has hundreds of competitors, most with years of domain authority. "Electrician Frankston" has a handful — and that's the search from someone who actually needs one in your area today.

  • Wrong keyword level. Suburb-level keywords are where local trades win. City-level terms are too competitive and attract the wrong traffic anyway — someone in Brunswick isn't calling a plumber based in Dandenong.
  • Incomplete Google Business Profile. Most tradies have claimed their profile but left it half-filled. An incomplete profile with no posts, no Q&A and no regular updates is treated by Google as a dormant business.
  • No review velocity. Eight reviews from three years ago won't win the Local Pack. Google weights recency heavily — a business collecting steady reviews every month will consistently outrank one that collected a bunch at launch and stopped.
  • Inconsistent NAP. Name, address and phone number needs to be identical across every directory, your website and your Google Business Profile. Variations confuse Google's local authority signals and dilute your ranking.

A plumber in Springvale with 60 reviews, an active Google Business Profile and suburb pages will beat a bigger plumbing business with a slick website and no local SEO. The bar is lower than it looks.
