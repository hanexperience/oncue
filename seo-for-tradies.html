(01)  How local SEO works

Four pillars that drive rankings.

Tradie SEO is not complicated. It's a specific set of activities done consistently over time.

01

Suburb targeting

Dedicated service pages for every suburb you want to rank in — Dandenong, Frankston, Cheltenham, Ringwood, Footscray and more — with real, unique content on each.

02

Google Business Profile

Your GBP is your most powerful local search asset. Correct category, complete service area, consistent NAP, regular posts and active review generation — all of it managed.

03

Review strategy

A systematic approach to generating and responding to Google reviews — the most direct ranking signal available to a local trade business, and the most visible trust signal to new customers.

04

On-page SEO

Correct title tags, H1 structure, schema markup, internal linking and NAP consistency across every page — the technical foundation that makes everything else work.

(02)  Where most tradies are losing

The four reasons tradie SEO fails.

Not because SEO doesn't work. Because these specific things are getting missed.

Google Business Profile is incomplete

Wrong or missing category, no service area set, no photos, no description. Google can't rank a profile it doesn't understand. Your GBP is the most important piece of your local search presence — treat it that way.

No suburb-specific pages

A single "Service Areas" page with a suburb list does nothing. Google needs a dedicated, indexed page for each suburb you want to rank in — with unique content, structured data and proper internal linking.

Ignoring reviews

Review quantity and recency are direct Local Pack ranking signals. A tradie with 4.9 stars and 80 reviews is going to outrank one with 4.9 stars and 6 reviews every single time. You need a system for generating them consistently.

Slow, unoptimised website

Page speed is a confirmed Google ranking factor. A slow site hurts your SEO before anyone reads a word. Most WordPress sites with page builders score below 50 on Google PageSpeed. That's a structural disadvantage in search.
