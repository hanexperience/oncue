What makes a tradie website actually work

There are five things a tradie website must do well. Most get one or two. The ones that win jobs get all five.

01

Load fast on mobile

Under two seconds. Most people searching for a tradie are on a phone, often in a stressful situation. A slow site gets abandoned immediately.

02

Phone number above the fold

Visible immediately, clickable to call. No scrolling required. This is the whole point — make it as easy as possible to call you.

03

Suburb-level SEO

Title tags, headings and copy that name your trade and the suburbs you work in — so Google can rank you for the searches that matter.

04

Real photos of your work

Stock photos don't build trust. Photos of your actual jobs, your team and your equipment tell a customer exactly what to expect when they hire you.

05

Google reviews displayed

A customer who's found you on Google and sees 50 genuine five-star reviews on your site will call you before they look at anyone else.

Why most tradie websites don't convert

  • Built for desktop, not mobile. The designer previewed it on a 27-inch monitor. Your customers are on iPhone screens in a hurry. These are very different experiences.
  • No SEO from the start. A good-looking site with no keyword targeting, no suburb pages and no structured data will rank for nothing. SEO needs to be designed in — not added later.
  • Generic copy. "We are a leading provider of quality services with a commitment to excellence." This says nothing and builds no trust. Customers want to know your trade, your suburb, how fast you come out and what it costs.
  • No social proof. A website without Google reviews, testimonials or photos of real work gives a customer no reason to choose you over a competitor they found at the same time.
  • Hard to contact. If the phone number requires scrolling to find, or the contact form is the only option, you're creating friction at the exact moment someone is ready to call.

WordPress or custom? What's right for you

Both options work well — the right choice depends on how much you want to manage your site yourself after launch.

Custom-built (HTML/CSS): Faster, lighter, no plugins to update, no security vulnerabilities from a CMS. Ideal if you want maximum performance and are happy to come back to Beets & Co for any updates. This is what this site is built on.

WordPress: A good choice if you want to add blog posts, update content or manage service pages yourself. I build WordPress sites that are just as fast as custom-built ones — no bloated page builders, no unnecessary plugins. For tradies who want to maintain their own site, this is usually the recommendation.
