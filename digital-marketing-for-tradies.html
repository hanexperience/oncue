If you've searched for digital marketing for tradies, you're probably tired of the same story: a monthly retainer, a generic strategy, and a report full of metrics that don't translate to jobs booked.

(01)  What it includes

What digital marketing for tradies actually means.

Digital marketing is a broad term. For trade businesses, only a handful of channels reliably produce results. Here's what moves the needle.

01

Local SEO

Ranking in the suburbs you work in — on Google Maps, in the Local Pack, and in organic results. This is where most tradie jobs come from online.

02

Website Design

Fast, mobile-first websites built to convert visitors into callers. Every page is structured to get someone to tap your number — not to win a design award.

03

Brand & Identity

A professional logo, colour system and visual identity that makes you look like the obvious choice before a customer reads a single word.

04

Content & Video

Job-site footage, before-and-afters, Google Business Profile posts. Content that builds trust, keeps your profile active and shows what you can do.

(03)  The common traps

Why most of it doesn't work.

Wrong keywords."Plumbing services" has thousands of competitors. "Plumber Dandenong" has a handful — and those are people who need someone right now.
Desktop-first websites.Over 70% of local service searches happen on mobile. A site that isn't fast on a phone loses jobs daily.
No review system.The Local Pack is won by review volume and recency. Most tradies have 6–10 reviews and no plan to grow them.
Chasing the wrong metrics.Impressions and follower counts don't pay the bills. Phone calls and quotes do.
(04)  What I do instead

The approach that actually works.

Suburb-first keyword strategy.I target the specific suburbs and job types your best customers search — not broad terms your competitors have owned for years.
Mobile-first by default.Everything I build loads fast on a phone. That's where your customers are when they need a tradie — not sitting at a desk.
A review system that runs itself.I set up automated post-job review requests so your Google Business Profile grows consistently — without you having to remember to ask.
Reported on calls and quotes.I report on what matters: phone calls, quote requests, and revenue. Not impressions, not reach, not follower counts.
