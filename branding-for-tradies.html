Most trade businesses have a logo. Few have a brand. The difference isn't aesthetics — it's whether your visual identity works hard enough to win trust at the moment a potential customer is deciding between you and the competitor they found at the same time.

Branding for tradies isn't about looking fancy. It's about looking credible. A customer comparing two plumbers on their phone in five seconds isn't reading case studies — they're making a gut-level decision based on how professional each business looks. A strong brand tips that decision in your favour before they've read anything.

I build visual identities specifically for trade businesses — logos, colour systems, typography and graphic design assets that work across your website, your vehicle signage, your quotes and your social media. Built by a senior creative director who understands what trade business customers respond to.

Your brand is the first thing a customer judges you on. Most tradies are competing against businesses that haven't updated their visual identity in a decade. The bar is low — and the opportunity is real.

(01)  What branding includes

Not just a logo. A complete system.

A proper brand identity for a trade business is a set of visual elements that work together consistently across everything you put in front of a customer.

01

Logo design

A professional logo built for your trade — designed to work at any size, in colour and black and white, on vehicles, workwear, websites and print.

02

Colour system

A primary and secondary palette chosen to stand out in your market, work on vehicles and workwear, and look credible on a website and in print.

03

Typography

A font pairing that communicates the right qualities — professional, reliable, established. Applied consistently across your website, documents and materials.

04

Graphic design assets

Quote templates, email signatures, business cards, social media templates and any other assets you need to show up professionally at every touchpoint.

(03)  Why it matters

Branding does things you can't see.

The tradies who dismiss branding are usually the ones competing on price. A strong brand is what lets you charge what your work is actually worth.

It wins the moment before the decision

On Google, in the Local Pack, on your website — a business that looks established and professional wins the trust of hesitant customers before they've called anyone.

It makes everything else work better

SEO, a new website, social media posts — all perform better when backed by a consistent, professional visual identity. Inconsistent branding undermines trust at every touchpoint.

It's the foundation of referrals

A customer who's proud of the company they hired recommends them to their mates. A professional brand makes your customers feel good about the choice they made.

It scales with your business

As you grow — more vehicles, more staff, bigger jobs — your brand scales with you. A strong identity built properly now doesn't need to be rebuilt when you're bigger.
