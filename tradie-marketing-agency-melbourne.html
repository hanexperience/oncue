(02)  Why tradie marketing usually fails

Four mistakes most tradies make.

Not because the tradies are bad at their job. Because the marketing advice they got wasn't built for their market.

Running ads before the website converts

Google Ads sends traffic to a website that doesn't convert. The tradie spends $2,000 on clicks that generate two enquiries. The problem isn't the ads — it's the destination. Fix the website first.

Treating SEO as an expense, not an investment

SEO compounds. The suburb page built in month one is still generating enquiries in year three. A tradie who stops their SEO retainer after three months because "it hasn't worked yet" has usually just abandoned results that were weeks away.

Ignoring brand

A professional brand doesn't just look better — it converts better. Customers comparing tradies on their phone make a gut-level trust decision based on how a business looks. A weak brand costs you jobs before a call is made.

Working with generalist agencies

A generalist agency that works with restaurants, e-commerce stores and real estate agents doesn't understand the decision cycle of a homeowner looking for a plumber in Cheltenham. Specialisation matters in marketing.
