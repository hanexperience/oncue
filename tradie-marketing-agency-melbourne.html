Why most tradie marketing doesn't work

The most common mistake trade businesses make is hiring a generalist agency on a monthly retainer. You pay $1,500–$3,000 a month, get a monthly report with charts, and never quite understand why the calls aren't coming in.

Here's what usually goes wrong:

  • The website isn't built for local search. It's a nice brochure that ranks for nothing because there's no suburb-specific content, no proper Google Business Profile, no structured data telling Google what you do and where.
  • The SEO is too broad. Nobody in Dandenong is searching for "plumbing services Australia." They're searching "plumber Dandenong" or "blocked drain Springvale." Targeting the right local keywords is the whole game.
  • There's no review strategy. Google's Local Pack — the three map results at the top of search — is won heavily by review volume and recency. A business with 40 genuine five-star reviews will beat a bigger business with 6.
  • The agency doesn't understand your customers. A tradie's customer is making a decision under time pressure, on their phone, between three local results. The agency that gets this — and builds marketing around it — is the one that moves the needle.
